# Starbucks Equity Valuation – Financial Statement Analysis

This project presents a comprehensive financial analysis and valuation of **Starbucks Corporation (NASDAQ: SBUX)** using financial statement analysis, Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) modelling, and the Dividend Discount Model (DDM).

The objective of the project is to estimate the **intrinsic value of Starbucks stock** and evaluate whether the company is fairly valued relative to its market price.

---

## Company Overview

Starbucks is one of the world’s largest premium coffee companies, operating across North America, international markets, and consumer packaged goods channels. The company benefits from strong brand equity, a large global store network, and a growing digital ecosystem.

The company continues to expand globally while focusing on operational efficiency, digital engagement, and shareholder returns through dividends and share buybacks.

---

## Financial Analysis

The project includes detailed financial analysis based on Starbucks’ historical financial statements.

### Revenue and Profitability Trends

Starbucks experienced strong revenue growth following the pandemic; however, recent years have seen pressure on margins due to rising labour costs, inflation in raw materials, and increased promotional spending. Profit margins have declined as operating costs increased and consumer demand softened in certain markets. 

### Cash Flow Analysis

The company generates strong operating cash flows supported by its global retail presence and premium product positioning. However, increased capital expenditures and shareholder returns have reduced free cash flow margins in recent periods. 

### Balance Sheet Analysis

Starbucks operates with a leveraged capital structure driven largely by debt financing and share buybacks. Although shareholder equity is negative due to capital return strategies, the company remains financially stable due to consistent cash flow generation. 

---

## Valuation Methodology

The intrinsic value of Starbucks was estimated using two primary valuation models.

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

Two DCF approaches were implemented:

* Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)
* Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)

Key assumptions used in the model include:

WACC: 8.49%
Cost of Equity: 9.34%
Terminal Growth Rate: 3.5%

Valuation results:

FCFF valuation: **$86.70 per share**
FCFE valuation: **$98.35 per share** 

---

### Dividend Discount Model (DDM)

A Dividend Discount Model was used as a secondary valuation approach to estimate the intrinsic value of the stock based on expected dividend growth.

Key assumptions:

Cost of Equity: 7.21%
Long-term Dividend Growth Rate: 4.5%

DDM valuation estimate:

**$91.29 per share** 

---

## Investment Recommendation

Based on the valuation models and financial analysis, the project assigns a **HOLD recommendation** for Starbucks stock.

The valuation suggests that Starbucks is **currently trading close to its intrinsic value**, with only modest upside potential.

Long-term growth may be supported by:

* International market expansion
* Digital ecosystem and loyalty programs
* Product innovation and store modernization

However, short-term risks include inflationary cost pressures, slower consumer traffic growth, and restructuring costs.
